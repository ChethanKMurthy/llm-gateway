# Product

## Register

brand (marketing landing + about wrap a live product console)

## Users
Senior AI/ML infrastructure engineers, platform/inference teams, and technical
recruiters evaluating the author. They arrive skeptical and fast-reading; they
can smell a fake demo and a templated landing page instantly. Context: a browser
tab opened from a portfolio link or a referral, often on a laptop, sometimes mid-
interview-loop.

## Product Purpose
An intelligent gateway that sits between an app and every LLM provider and makes
each request cheaper, faster, safer, and observable — semantic caching, dynamic
model routing, cost/latency prediction, security guardrails, and a reinforcement-
learning router. The site must (1) explain the *process* clearly, (2) prove it is
real and running, and (3) feel like it was built by someone with taste and rigor.

## Brand Personality
Precise · warm · quietly confident. The voice of a great systems engineer who
also writes well: exact, unhurried, never hypey. Shows the mechanism instead of
claiming outcomes. Honest about what's real vs simulated — that honesty IS the
flex for this audience.

## Anti-references
- Generic dark admin-SaaS dashboards (Linear/Vercel clones, endless card grids).
- Crypto / "web3" gradient-glow, glassmorphism, gradient text, neon hype.
- The cream/beige "tasteful AI editorial" template and display-serif magazine look.
- Corporate-navy enterprise B2B stock-photo soullessness.

## Design Principles
1. **Show the mechanism.** A visitor should *watch* a request flow through the
   gateway, not read adjectives about it. The animated pipeline is the hero.
2. **Practice what it preaches.** A page about fast, efficient infrastructure must
   itself be fast, light, and precise. Self-hosted fonts, no CDN, no bloat.
3. **Calibrated honesty.** Real numbers with their caveats. Reviewers trust the
   person who says "20–45% in real traffic," not "95% guaranteed."
4. **Warmth carries it.** Clay accent + warm paper + human copy keep a deeply
   technical product inviting rather than cold.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large/labels ≥3:1. Full `prefers-reduced-motion`
alternative for every animation (canvas pauses, reveals show instantly). Keyboard-
operable controls; visible focus.
